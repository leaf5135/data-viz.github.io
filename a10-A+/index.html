A10 (Redesigned) Sequential Bar Chart

Distribution of Diseases (1968)

Data Source: Health Dataset from CORGIS Project



This chart is better because it uses a color scale that goes in order to show the number of infections. This helps the reader see the infection numbers
without just looking at how tall the bars are. It also includes a legend, so readers can understand what the colors mean. Plus, there's a footer that
tells you where the data came from. And the annotations on the chart are clearer, making it easier to understand.
